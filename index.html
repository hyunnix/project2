Tracing Consumer Patterns through Discarded Receipts

↳ This project seeks to explore the theme of tracing by collecting and analyzing discarded receipts from the streets of New York City, specifically around the bustling intersection of 33rd Street and 6th Avenue. By tracing the journeys of these seemingly mundane pieces of paper, we uncover a hidden narrative of consumer behavior, urban patterns, and the lifecycle of everyday transactions.

↳ Receipts, often discarded without thought, are tangible traces of personal interactions with the urban environment. Each receipt carries with it a wealth of information: locations, purchase types, times of day, and even behavioral habits of consumers. By examining these fragments of daily life, we aim to understand more about the consumer landscape of this specific area and how individuals navigate commercial spaces in NYC. This project considers how everyday objects like receipts, often overlooked, reflect the intricate patterns of human activity in a dense urban ecosystem.

↳ Additionally, the project highlights broader environmental concerns about waste and the life of disposable materials in city settings. By tracing the life cycle of receipts, from purchase to disposal, we engage in a conversation about sustainability, urban waste, and the impact of consumer culture. This exploration offers insights into both the human and material dimensions of city life, presenting receipts as key artifacts of our everyday interactions with design and urban infrastructure.

↳ Ultimately, this project seeks to turn attention to the ways in which discarded objects can trace the invisible footprints of city dwellers and provide a window into the rhythms of urban living in one of the most vibrant areas of New York City.
